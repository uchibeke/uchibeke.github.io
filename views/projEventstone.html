EVENTSTONE

I believe that people should be able to do something meaningful and unique with the limited resources that they have. I also believe that we should be able to restrict access to the information about the things we do, like the events we organize, and the people we do it with, like the guests who attend our events. That is one reason why I created Eventstone.

MY ROLE


Eventstone is an independent project. I worked on all the design and implementation. I, however, talked to a lot of prospective users to get their insight and then I tailored my focus to their need.

Experience Strategy and Vision

I created frameworks and prototypes to test the vision, design principles and content strategy. This helped decide the features that were worth implementing and those that were not worthwhile.

Planning

I defined the product by posing questions to event organizers. I focused on the user goals and balanced this with long term goals of Eventstone. I prioritised simplicity and storage of data in users local device. The data is stored in local storage.

Design and Execution

I implemeted Eventstone for both mobile and desktop computers. I created wireframes, prototypes and design specs. This all started with simple white board sketches. Next, I did the coding. For the coding aspect of Eventstone, I planned out and designed algorithms that were efficient for the purposes that they serve. An example of this is the algorithm that formats names copied from multiple columns of an excel file into first and last name and adds them as elements, on at a time, to the guest list.

Testing

As at January 8th, 2016, I have test run Eventstone at three events. Testing it with actual users was very beneficial to the design of Eventstone. I made refinements and added new features based on user feedback.

THE CHALLENGE

Provide a system that will save peoples time, empower event organizers especially those organizing small events and protect the privacy of guests.

At events, I notice guests waiting in line to be checked in from a list on paper or, on rare occasions, a document on a computer. The process takes time and, I imagine, it must be stressful for some guests. This, as you might be aware, is also not enviroment-friendly.

My passion for making things that solve my problems and the problems of my friends and also my other believes are the driving force for the creation of Eventstone

SOLUTIONS AND RESULTS

User Feedback is always the best feedback.

When I first started designing Eventstone, I was missing a lot of details about the features that needs to be implemented like the ability to print out a name badge for a guest who is added to Eventstone during the event or the ability to do the check-in simulteneously on different devices. I learned about these key features by talking to Users.

Results

Eventstone is currently being used in my university by student groups. I am looking forward to eventstone being used by more Universities around Canada.
